About Geometric Data Analysis Reading Group

We are a group of faculties and students in the Department of Statistics in the University of Washington interested in Geometric Data Analysis. Our research topics focus on analyzing the underlying geometric structure of data. You can add yourself to the group mailing list at https://mailman12.u.washington.edu/mailman/listinfo/geometry

You can see the announcements about the group in the News tab. We will read and discuss various papers and themes of Geometric Data Analysis such as

  • Topological Data analysis
  • manifold learning algorithms
  • intrinsic dimension estimation
  • dimension reduction
  • the Laplacian: what it tells us about the data, how to estimate it

Everyone is welcome to join! For student participants: If you plan to volunteer for a presentation or leading a discussion, you can sign up for 1 stat 600 credit with one of the faculty organizers.

Organizers: Marina Meila , Yen-Chi Chen, Jerry Wei, James Buenfil
